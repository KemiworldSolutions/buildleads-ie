# Dublin buyer segments — who to email and where to find them

Order is by my read of best risk/reward. Hit the first three before touching the others.

## 1. Solar PV installers (best first target)
**Why:** SEAI grant scheme means every solar installer in Dublin is fighting for the same homeowners. A targeted weekly list of every Dublin household that just filed a planning application for an extension or new roof is a *qualified* lead — those people are about to spend money on the house and PV is an obvious add-on. SEAI also publishes a list of registered installers, so finding 30 emails is trivial.

**Where to find them:**
- SEAI registered installer list: https://www.seai.ie/grants/home-energy-grants/find-a-registered-company/
- Filter by Dublin postcode
- Pull name + website + public contact email

**Pitch angle:** "Every Dublin homeowner who filed a planning application this week. They're already spending. Be the first PV quote in their inbox."

**Pricing:** €49/mo, weekly delivery. Solar installer ACV is high enough to absorb this.

## 2. Roofing contractors
**Why:** Every extension, every loft conversion, every new build needs a roofer. Roofers are notoriously underserved by lead-gen because the existing aggregators (CIS/BCI) are priced for main contractors, not sole-trader roofers.

**Where to find them:**
- Golden Pages: goldenpages.ie/q/business/dublin/roofing
- Construction Industry Federation member directory
- Google Maps "roofers near Dublin" — filter to verified businesses with a website

**Pitch:** "Every loft conversion and extension filed in Dublin this week. Skip the bidding war."

**Pricing:** €29/mo. Roofer margins are thinner; meet the price.

## 3. Structural engineers (small practices)
**Why:** Any extension over single-storey or any structural alteration in Dublin needs a structural engineer's certification. Small practices (1–5 person) need a steady drip of new work and don't have a BD function.

**Where to find them:**
- Engineers Ireland directory: engineersireland.ie (search "structural", filter Dublin)
- Look for Chartered Engineers at firms with <10 staff

**Pitch:** "Every new Dublin extension that needs your stamp, in your inbox every Monday."

**Pricing:** €49/mo.

## 4. Kitchen & extension fitters
**Where:** Houzz Ireland, Instagram (search #dublinkitchens, #dublinextensions for active businesses), Golden Pages.
**Pitch:** "Pre-qualified Dublin extension leads — they're already at planning stage."

## 5. Architects (small practices looking for sub-work)
**Where:** RIAI member directory: riai.ie
**Note:** Many architects are *agents* on the very lists you're scraping, which gives you a perfect opener: "I see you're the listed agent on 3456/24 in Drumcondra. I run a service that..."

## 6. Solicitors handling planning objections
**Where:** Law Society of Ireland directory.
**Note:** This is a slower-converting but higher-LTV segment. Hold for month 2.

## 7. Insurance brokers (renovation insurance)
**Where:** Brokers Ireland directory.
**Note:** Niche. Test only after the first three segments are validated.

## Hard rules

1. Use the company's published role address (info@, contact@, sales@) or a publicly listed named address. Do NOT scrape personal Gmail addresses.
2. Always include a one-click unsubscribe.
3. Maximum 30 emails per day to start. You are building a business, not running a spam operation.
4. After 30 emails per segment, STOP and look at responses before sending more.
